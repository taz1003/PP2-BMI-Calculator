BMI & BMR

Body Mass Index (BMI) and Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) are two essential metrics used to assess body composition and metabolism, often forming the basis of health and wellness evaluations.

Body Mass Index (BMI)

BMI is a measurement that compares a person's weight to their height, providing a general indication of body fat and helping to classify individuals into various weight categories. The formula for BMI divides a person's weight in kilograms by their height in meters squared (kg/m²). BMI is used to categorize individuals into classes based on standard health ranges:
Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
Normal weight: BMI between 18.5 and 24.9
Overweight: BMI between 25 and 29.9
Obesity: BMI 30 and above (subdivided into Class I, II, and III for more severe obesity levels)
While BMI offers a helpful overview, it does not account for differences in muscle mass, bone density, or fat distribution, which can vary significantly between individuals.

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)

BMR represents the number of calories your body needs to maintain essential functions—such as breathing, circulation, and cell production—while at rest. This metric reflects the energy required to keep the body functioning in a non-active state. BMR is influenced by factors like age, gender, body composition, and genetics. Understanding your BMR can be a valuable tool in planning calorie intake and energy expenditure for weight management and health.

Formula to calculate BMR:
Male: BMR = (10 x weight in kg) + (6.25 x height in cm) - (5 x age in years) + 5
Female BMR = (10 x weight in kg) + (6.25 x height in cm) - (5 x age in years) - 161


Together, BMI and BMR offer a broader understanding of health and metabolic needs, helping individuals to tailor their nutrition, exercise, and lifestyle choices.
